# HR-Overtime-Analytics-Dashboard
Workforce analytics dashboard designed to uncover overtime trends, employee utilization patterns, and operational workforce insights through interactive Power BI reporting

## Objective
To analyze employee overtime trends, identify workforce utilization patterns, evaluate overtime distribution across employee groups and time periods, and provide actionable insights that support workforce planning and operational efficiency

## Tools & Techniques
- **Tools Used:** Power BI , DAX, Microsoft Excel, SQL, Power Query
- Consolidated employee overtime and workforce data to provide a comprehensive view of overtime utilization across the organization.
- Created workforce performance metrics to evaluate overtime trends, employee participation, and staffing demands over time.
- Analyzed overtime patterns across demographic groups, work periods, and organizational dimensions to uncover workforce insights.
- Enabled dynamic workforce exploration through interactive filtering, drill-through analysis, and self-service reporting capabilities.
- Developed executive-ready visualizations to support workforce planning, resource allocation, and operational decision-making.
- Identified peak overtime periods and workforce utilization patterns to highlight opportunities for scheduling optimization and cost management.

## Key Insights
- Employees recorded a total of **493,604 overtime instances**, highlighting significant overtime utilization across the workforce.
- The organization accumulated approximately **1.48 million total overtime hours**, indicating substantial operational demand beyond regular working hours.
- **Morning shifts generated the highest overtime volume**, with **176,712 overtime occurrences**, significantly exceeding all other periods of the day.
- **Noon shifts recorded the lowest overtime activity, with only 35,145 overtime occurrences**, suggesting lower staffing pressure during this period.
- Overtime distribution was relatively balanced across marital status categories, with divorced employees accounting for approximately **21.78%** of overtime hours.
- Overtime trends remained relatively consistent across years, indicating stable workforce demand patterns

## Business Impact
- Identified peak overtime periods to support workforce planning and scheduling decisions.
- Highlighted opportunities to optimize staffing levels during high-demand morning shifts.
- Enabled HR teams to monitor overtime utilization and reduce potential employee burnout risks.
- Supported data-driven decision-making through interactive workforce analytics and reporting.
